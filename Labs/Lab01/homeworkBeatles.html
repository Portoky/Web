Presenting... The Beatles

Article by János Ákos-Márton ©

The Beatles were an English rock band formed in Liverpool in 1960, comprising

  • John Lennon
  • Paul McCartney
  • George Harrison
  • Ringo Starr

They are regarded as the most influential band of all time and were integral to the development of 1960s counterculture and the recognition of popular music as an art form. Rooted in skiffle, beat and 1950s rock 'n' roll, their sound incorporated elements of classical music and traditional pop in innovative ways.
The band also explored music styles ranging from folk and Indian music to psychedelia and hard rock. As pioneers in recording, songwriting and artistic presentation, the Beatles revolutionized many aspects of the music industry and were often publicized as leaders of the era's youth and sociocultural movements.

Band members

John Lennon

With a voice that could stir souls and lyrics that questioned reality, John Lennon was the poetic provocateur who turned melody into revolution.

Paul McCartney

The maestro of melodies and the quintessential showman, Paul McCartney charmed the world with his bass lines and ballads, forever igniting Beatlemania's flame

George Harrison

From the quiet corners of Liverpool to the spiritual depths of Indian philosophy, George Harrison's guitar mastery and mystic musings painted a kaleidoscope of sound and soul.

Ringo Starr

With a backbeat that kept the world swinging and a wit as quick as his drum fills, Ringo Starr was the heartbeat of The Beatles, bringing rhythm and laughter in equal measure.
